30 Description
31  Implementation of the k-omega-SST turbulence model for compressible flows.
32 
33  Turbulence model described in
34  \verbatim
35  Menter, F., Esch, T.,
36  "Elements of Industrial Heat Transfer Prediction",
37  16th Brazilian Congress of Mechanical Engineering (COBEM),
38  Nov. 2001.
39  \endverbatim
40 
41  with updated coefficients from
42  \verbatim
43  Menter, F. R., Kuntz, M., and Langtry, R.,
44  "Ten Years of Industrial Experience with the SST Turbulence Model",
45  Turbulence, Heat and Mass Transfer 4, 2003,
46  pp. 625 - 632.
47  \endverbatim
48 
49  but with the consistent production terms from the 2001 paper as form in the
50  2003 paper is a typo, see
51  \verbatim
52  http://turbmodels.larc.nasa.gov/sst.html
53  \endverbatim
54 
55  and the addition of the optional F3 term for rough walls from
56  \verbatim
57  Hellsten, A.
58  "Some Improvements in Menter’s k-omega-SST turbulence model"
59  29th AIAA Fluid Dynamics Conference,
60  AIAA-98-2554,
61  June 1998.
62  \endverbatim
63 
64  Note that this implementation is written in terms of alpha diffusion
65  coefficients rather than the more traditional sigma (alpha = 1/sigma) so
66  that the blending can be applied to all coefficuients in a consistent
67  manner. The paper suggests that sigma is blended but this would not be
68  consistent with the blending of the k-epsilon and k-omega models.
69 
70  Also note that the error in the last term of equation (2) relating to
71  sigma has been corrected.
72 
73  The default model coefficients correspond to the following:
74  \verbatim
75  kOmegaSSTCoeffs
76  {
77  alphaK1 0.85;
78  alphaK2 1.0;
79  alphaOmega1 0.5;
80  alphaOmega2 0.856;
81  beta1 0.075;
82  beta2 0.0828;
83  betaStar 0.09;
84  gamma1 5/9;
85  gamma2 0.44;
86  a1 0.31;
87  b1 1.0;
88  c1 10.0;
89  F3 no;
90  Prt 1.0;
91  }
92  \endverbatim
