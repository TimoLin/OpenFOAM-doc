25  Foam::compressible::RASModels::v2f

27 Group
28  grpCmpRASTurbulence

30 Description
31  Lien and Kalitzin's v2-f turbulence model for incompressible flows, with
32  a limit imposed on the turbulent viscosity given by Davidson et al.
33 
34  The model solves for turbulence k and epsilon, with additional equations
35  for the turbulence stress normal to streamlines, v2, and elliptic damping
36  function, f. The variant implemented employs N=6, such that f=0 on walls.
37 
38  Wall boundary conditions are:
39 
40  k = kLowReWallFunction
41  epsilon = epsilonLowReWallFunction
42  v2 = v2WallFunction
43  f = fWallFunction
44 
45  These are applicable to both low- and high-Reynolds number flows.
46 
47  Inlet values can be approximated by:
48 
49  v2 = 2/3 k
50  f = zero-gradient
51 
52 
53  References:
54 
55  Lien F-S, Kalitzin G, 2001. Computations of transonic flow with the v2-f
56  turbulence model. Int. J. Heat Fluid Flow 22, pp 53-61
57 
58  Davidson L, Nielsen P, Sveningsson A, 2003. Modifications of the v2-f
59  model for computing the flow in a 3D wall jet. Turbulence, Heat and Mass
60  Transfer 4, pp 577-584
61 
62  The default model coefficients are given as:
63  \verbatim
64  v2fCoeffs
65  {
66  Cmu 0.22;
67  CmuKEps 0.09;
68  C1 1.4;
69  C2 0.3;
70  CL 0.23;
71  Ceta 70;
72  Ceps2 1.9;
73  sigmaEps 1.3;
74  sigmaK 1;
75  }
76  \endverbatim
77 
78 Note
79  If the kLowReWallFunction is employed, a velocity variant of the turbulent
80  viscosity wall function should be used, e.g. nutUWallFunction. Turbulence
81  k variants (nutk...) for this case will not behave correctly.
82
